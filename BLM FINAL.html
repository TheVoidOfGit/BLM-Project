African American Inventors

Did you know that many of the devices we use today were made by brilliant inventors but some of the best inventions were made by african american inventors, so in this website I am going to talk to you about african american inventors, starting with

Thomas L. Jennings

Thomas L. Jennings invented a new way to dry-clean clothes in 1821. Even though people argued that slaves weren't allowed to own a patent for their inventions, Thomas was born a free man and became the first African American patent holder in the United States and was able to gain his inventions rights and profit from it.

Thomas L. Jennings helped us by finding a new and better way to clean clothes but this next inventor created something that saves thousands of lives and he is

Charles Drew

Charles Drew was an African-American physician known for his work in blood plasma preservation. Blood plasma helps boost the a patient's blood volume, which can prevent shock, and can help treat blood clotting, so Drew's research into the storage, processing, and shipment of blood plasma helped save the lives of hundreds of people during World War II, and still after all this time it continues to save lives today. In 1941, Drew became the first director of the American Red Cross Blood Bank in New York.

Charles helped save lives by making blood plasma more accessible and the next inventor also helps save live and he is

Garret Morgan

Garret Morgan was born in Kentucky in 1877 and was the first black man in Cleveland to own a car. When he was just 14 years old, he moved north to Ohio. Morgan worked on his mechanical skills and Then, in 1923, He got the idea to add a third light as a warning instead of just having “Stop” and “Go.”He got the idea after he witnessed an accident at a corner, and got the idea: If he designed an automated signal with an interim “warning” position, then it would help prepare people to slow to a stop rather then a sudden stop. Unfortunatly now people use it as a race to see if they can beat the yellow light.
